Why convert images to PDF?

A great many Indian admission portals, scholarship forms and government recruitment sites insist that supporting papers — Aadhaar, mark sheets, certificates, photos — arrive as one single PDF. When those live as loose JPG/PNG files, converting to PDF is the fastest route to meeting the requirement.

PDF also holds up better than a plain image: it locks in the layout, survives sharing without losing clarity, and opens in practically every portal and email client.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert an image to PDF online for free?
Add your JPG or PNG pictures to the tool above and hit Convert to PDF — the file downloads straight away, with no sign-up.
Can I combine multiple images into one PDF?
Yes — load several pictures and each becomes its own page, and you can drop any of them before converting.
Is my image uploaded to a server?
No. The jsPDF library does all the work inside your browser, so your images never leave your device.
What image formats are supported?
JPG, JPEG, PNG and WEBP, with each picture placed on a single A4 page of the resulting PDF.
Why do portals ask for documents as PDF?
PDF opens everywhere and survives printing or sharing better than an image, which is why government and university portals favour it for uploads such as Aadhaar, certificates and marksheets.
What size is the output PDF page?
Every picture is scaled onto an A4 page (210×297 mm) with its original aspect ratio kept intact.
