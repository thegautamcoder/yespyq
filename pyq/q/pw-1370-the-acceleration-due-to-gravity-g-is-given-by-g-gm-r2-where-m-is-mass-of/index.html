Assertion (A): With the increase of temperature, the viscosity of glycerine increases. ​ Reason (R): Rise of temperature increases kinetic energy of molecules.Codes:

a​Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A
bBoth A and R are true but R is not a correct explanation of A ​
cA is true but R is false ​
dA is false but R is true
✓ Correct answer: d) A is false but R is true
ExplanationViscosity is a measure of a fluid›s resistance to flow.It describes the internal friction of a moving fluid.Glycerine is a viscous liquid, meaning it has a relatively high resistance to flow at room temperature.Assertion (A) is false: The viscosity of liquids generally decreases with an increase in temperature.This is because higher temperatures lead to increased molecular motion, which weakens the intermolecular forces that cause viscosity.Reason (R) is true: Kinetic energy is the energy of motion.When the temperature of a substance increases, the molecules gain more kinetic energy, causing them to move faster.225
