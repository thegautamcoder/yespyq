Consider the following statements:1. The Barren Island volcano is an active volcano located in Indian territory.2. Barren Island lies about 140 km east of Great Nicobar.3. The last time the Barren Island volcano erupted was in 1991 and it has remained inactive since then.Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

a1 only
b2 and 3
c3 only
d1 and 3
✓ Correct answer: a) 1 only
ExplanationStatement 1 is correct: Barren Island is India’s only active volcano and is located in the Andaman Sea, within the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.It belongs to the volcanic arc of the Sunda Trench and is part of the Andaman Subduction Zone which makes it seismically active.Statement 2 is incorrect: Barren Island is located about 140 km northeast of Port Blair (the capital of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands), not Great Nicobar.Great Nicobar is located far south of the Andaman Islands, whereas Barren Island is in the northern Andaman Sea.Statement 3 is incorrect: The Barren Island volcano has erupted multiple times after 1991. Significant eruptions were recorded in 1994–1995, 2005, 2009, and most recently in 2017–2018. Thus, it has not remained inactive since 1991. SUPER HINT When S1 says its active volcano and S3 says its inactive since approx 27 years, that means both statements clearly contradicts.Its a active volcano is a well known fact as being the "only" active one in India, thus S3 is more likely false, giving option A as correct.228
