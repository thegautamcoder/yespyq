With reference to Indian freedom struggle, consider the following events:1. Mutiny in Royal Indian Navy2. Quit India Movement launched3. Second Round Table Conference What is the correct chronological sequence of the above events?

a1, 2, 3
b2, 1. 3
c3, 2, 1
d3, 1, 2
✓ Correct answer: c) 3, 2, 1
ExplanationSecond Round Table Conference was held in London, between September 7, 1931 to December 1, 1931. The Indian National Congress nominated Gandhi as its sole representative.The Quit India Movement, launched by Mahatma Gandhi in August 1942, called for an immediate end to British rule in India.It was a mass civil disobedience movement, marked by protests, strikes, and widespread arrests.The Royal Navy Mutiny of 1946, also known as the Naval Uprising, involved Indian sailors protesting against harsh conditions, low wages, and discrimination in the British Royal Navy.The mutiny spread across several naval bases, becoming a significant part of the larger Indian independence movement.112
