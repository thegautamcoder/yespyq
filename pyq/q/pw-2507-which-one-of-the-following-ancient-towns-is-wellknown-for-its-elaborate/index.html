Which one of the following ancient towns is wellknown for its elaborate system of water arvesting and management by building a series of dams and channelizing water into connected reservoirs? (2021)

aRopar
bRakhigarhi
cKalibangan
dDholavira
✓ Correct answer: d) Dholavira
ExplanationAns.(a) Exp: Indus Valley Civilization featured advanced town planning with well-laid-out streets, drainage systems, and standardized brick construction.Notable examples include the cities of Mohenjo-Daro, with its grid-like streets and Great Bath, and Harappa, known for its drainage systems and carefully planned residential areas.Option (a) is correct: Discovered in 1968 by Jagat Pati Joshi, Dholavira is one of the best-preserved Indus Valley sites, with a unique water management system using reservoirs and dams.Located between the Manhar and Mandsar streams, it was likely accessible by boat when the Rann’s water levels were higher.Options (b), (c) and (d) are incorrect: Kalibangan is located on the bank of the Ghaggar river in Rajasthan.A fire altar and wooden plough have been found here.Rakhigarhi is the largest Harappan site in the Indian subcontinent.Planned townships having mud-brick as well as burnt-brick were found here.At the Ropar site, a building made of stone, a dog buried with humans and an oval pit burial have been found.Additional Information : Indus Valley Site Dhol avira Known for an advanced water management system with reservoirs and dams;
