The native state of Tripura became involved in the freedom movement early in the 20th century because:

athe kings of Tripura were always anti-British
bthe Bengal revolutionaries took shelter in Tripura
cthe tribes of the state were fiercely freedom loving
dthere were already some groups fighting against the kingship and its protector, the British
✓ Correct answer: d) there were already some groups fighting against the kingship and its protector, the British
ExplanationThe native state of Tripura, a princely state during British rule, became involved in the freedom movement due to its geographical proximity to Bengal and the political dynamics of the early 20th century.The native state of Tripura became part of the freedom movement early in the 20th century because some groups in the state were against the king’s rule and his support from the British.They wanted to challenge the king’s power and reduce British influence in the region.These efforts were linked to the larger fight for India’s independence, inspired by the freedom movement in nearby Bengal and other areas.155
