🏛️ History & Art-CultureUPSC 1998Gupta Period

Many of the Greeks, Kushanas, and Shakas embraced Buddhism rather than Hinduism because:

aBuddhism was in the ascendant at that time
bThey had renounced the policy of war and violence
cCaste-ridden Hinduism did not attract them
dBuddhism provided easier access to Indian society
✓ Correct answer: c) Caste-ridden Hinduism did not attract them
ExplanationThe Greeks (2nd century BCE to 1st century CE in northwestern India), Kushanas (1st to 3rd century CE across northern India and Central Asia), and Shakas (2nd century BCE to 4th century CE in western India) were key powers during the Post-Mauryan period.Greek, Kushanas and Shakas needed legitimacy for their rule for people to accept them as their king.Accepting religion in the local area was the best way to achieve legitimacy.Buddhism provided the easiest entry in comparison to Hinduism which had strict birth related caste structure.38
