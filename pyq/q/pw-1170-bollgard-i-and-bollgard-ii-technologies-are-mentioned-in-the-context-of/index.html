Bollgard I and Bollgard II technologies are mentioned in the context of:

aClonal propagation of crop plants
bDeveloping genetically modified crop plants
cProduction of plant growth substances
dProduction of biofertilizers
✓ Correct answer: b) Developing genetically modified crop plants
ExplanationBollgard I and Bollgard II are genetically modified (GM) cotton varieties developed by Monsanto (now part of Bayer) to provide resistance to bollworm, a major pest of cotton plants.Option (b) is correct: Bollgard I was the first genetically modified cotton variety which was engineered with a gene from the bacterium Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt).This gene enables the cotton plant to produce a toxin that is harmful to bollworms thereby offering pest resistance.Bollgard II is an advanced version which incorporates two Bt genes (Cry1Ac and Cry2Ab) and provides broader protection against a wider range of pests, including the pink bollworm.These genetically modified crops are designed to reduce pesticide dependency and enhance crop yields by making the plants resistant to pests that typically damage cotton bolls.9
