🏛️ History & Art-CultureUPSC 1998British Policies in India

“The Congress is tottering to its fall and one of my great ambitions while in India, is to assist it to a peaceful demise. “This statement is attributed to:

aLord Dufferin
bLord Curzon
cLord Lytton
dNone of the above
✓ Correct answer: b) Lord Curzon
ExplanationThe statement is attributed to Lord Curzon, who served as the Viceroy of India from 1899 to 1905. This remark reflects his deep opposition to the Indian National Congress (INC) and his belief in maintaining British control over India.Appointment of Police Commission (1902) under Sir Andrew Frazer to review police administration, Partition of Bengal (1905) and curbing press freedom through Official Secrets Act (1904) were other instances of Lord Curzon’s suppressing acts during his tenure.While Curzon believed his policies would weaken the Congress, many of his actions galvanized the nationalist movement instead.The backlash led to the Swadeshi Movement and laid the groundwork for a more united and organized fight for Indian independence.183
