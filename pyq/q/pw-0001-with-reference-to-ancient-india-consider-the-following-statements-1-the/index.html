With reference to ancient India, consider the following statements:1. The concept of Stupa is Buddhist in origin.2. Stupa was generally a repository of relics.3. Stupa was a votive and commemorative structure in Buddhist tradition.How many of the statements given above are correct?

aOnly one
bOnly two
cAll three
dNone of the above
✓ Correct answer: b) Only two
ExplanationStatement 1 is incorrect : The concept of stupa originates from pre-Buddhist burial mounds in India which were used for funerary purposes such as burial mounds called 'Megalithic cairns'.Buddhism adopted and transformed these existing structures into significant religious monuments.While the architectural form of the Stupa was adapted and refined by Buddhists, its origins are not exclusively Buddhist.Statement 2 is correct : Stupas were primarily built to serve as repositories of sacred relics.These relics could include the ashes or bodily remains of the Buddha, his disciples, or other revered figures.Example: the Dhamek Stupa at Sarnath (Uttar Pradesh) is believed to mark the spot where the Buddha delivered his first sermon.The relics were placed in a small chamber called the 'harmika' at the center of the stupa, making it a focal point for veneration and pilgrimage.Statement 3 is correct : Stupas serve both votive and commemorative functions often constructed as acts of reverence and offerings to the Buddha or other enlightened figures.They are built to honor important even
