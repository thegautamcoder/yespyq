​After consolidating his power, Balban assumed the grand title of:

a​Tute-Hind
bKaisr-I-Hind
c​Zil-I-Ilahi
d​Din-I-Ilahi Medieval History 59
✓ Correct answer: c) ​Zil-I-Ilahi
ExplanationBalban (reigned 1266–1287) was one of the most prominent rulers of the Delhi Sultanate, particularly known for his strong and centralized rule.He was part of the Slave dynasty which began with Qutb-ud-Din Aibak.After consolidating his power, Balban assumed the grand title of "Zil-i-Ilahi", which means "The Shadow of God" or "The Shadow of the Divine." This title was a reflection of his absolute authority and divine right to rule.By adopting this title, Balban sought to emphasize the sacred nature of his rule and his unquestionable authority, essentially equating his position with the will of God.3.
