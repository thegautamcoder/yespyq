🌱 Environment & EcologyUPSC 2011Sustainable Development

Human activities in the recent past have caused the increased concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, but a lot of it does not remain in the lower atmosphere because of1. Its escape into the outer stratosphere.2. The photosynthesis by phytoplankton in the oceans:3. The trapping of air in the polar ice caps.Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

a1 and 2
b2 only
c2 and 3
d3 only UPSC GS Paper-I PYQs 498
✓ Correct answer: b) 2 only
ExplanationFor slowing down global warming, carbon sequestra- tion involves the capture and storage of CO2. It is captured from the air, industries or power stations and stored permanently underground.This promotes Long-term reserve of CO2 or forms of carbon to control temperatures.Carbon sequestration aids in lowering the amount of GHGs (due to combustion of fossil fuels) present in the air.Statement 2 is correct: The photosynthesis by phytoplankton in the oceans is natural carbon sequestration and it is responsible for much of CO2 being moved from lower atmosphere to oceans: Statements 1 is incorrect: CO₂ is a heavy gas that primarily ac- cumulates in the troposphere and lower stratosphere but does not escape into the outer stratosphere or space.The primary removal mechanisms of CO₂ involve absorption by oceans, pho- tosynthesis by plants, and geological sequestration rather than atmospheric escape.Statements 3 is incorrect: Polar ice caps and glaciers are not a major active carbon sink.Ice trapping does not remove a signif- icant amount of CO₂ from the atmosphere on short time scales.
