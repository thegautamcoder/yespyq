Consider the following statements:1. In the tropical zone, the western sections of the oceans are warmer than the eastern sections owing to the influence of trade winds.2. In the temperate zone, westerlies make the eastern sections of oceans warmer than the western sections.Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

a1 only
b2 only
cBoth 1 and 2
dNeither 1 nor 2
✓ Correct answer: c) Both 1 and 2
ExplanationStatement 1 is correct : In the tropical zone, trade winds blow from the east towards the west.These winds push warm surface water towards the western parts of the oceans (e.g., the western Pacific Ocean and the western Atlantic Ocean).As a result, the water in the western parts of tropical oceans is warmer than in the eastern parts.The eastern sections of the oceans tend to have upwelling, where colder, nutrient-rich waters from deeper layers rise to the surface.Statement 2 is correct : In the temperate zone, the westerlies (winds blowing from the west towards the east) push the surface water eastward, causing the western parts of oceans (like the western coast of continents) to be colder.The water in the eastern sections of oceans tends to be warmer due to the Ekman transport, which moves warm water towards the eastern coasts.For example, in the North Atlantic Ocean, the Gulf Stream carries warm water from the tropics to the eastern coasts of North America and Europe, keeping the eastern sections warmer than the western parts.28
