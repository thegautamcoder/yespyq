Consider the following statements: The Government of India Act, 1935 provided for:1. the provincial autonomy.2. the establishment of a Federal court.3. All India Federation at the centre.Which of the statements given above are correct?

a1 and 2
b2 and 3
c1 and 3
d1, 2 and 3
✓ Correct answer: d) 1, 2 and 3
ExplanationGovernment of India Act, 1935, which introduced significant constitutional reforms.All the three given statements are correct: The Government of India Act, 1935 provided for:Provincial autonomy, granting greater powers to provincial legislatures.The establishment of a Federal Court to resolve disputes between the central and provincial governments.The creation of an All-India Federation at the center, though it was never fully implemented.The expansion of the electorate, allowing more Indians to vote.The introduction of a bicameral legislature at the center, consisting of the Council of States and the Federal Assembly.141
