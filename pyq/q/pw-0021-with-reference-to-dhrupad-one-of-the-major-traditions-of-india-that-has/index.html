With reference to Dhrupad, one of the major traditions of India that has been kept alive for centuries, Which of the following statements are correct?1. Dhrupad originated and developed in the Rajput kingdoms during the Mughal period2. Dhrupad is primarily a devotional and spiritual music3. Dhrupad alap uses Sanskrit syllables from Mantras.Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

a1 and 2 only
b2 and 3 only
c1, 2 and 3
dNone of the above is correct.
✓ Correct answer: b) 2 and 3 only
ExplanationDhrupad is derived from ‘Dhruva’ (fixed star) and ‘Pada’ (song), is an ancient form of classical music that emerged from early vocal traditions.It is performed with tanpura and pakhawaj, its lyrics in Braj Bhasha often reflect devotion and heroism, expressing emotions like veera and sringar.Key gharanas include Dagarvani, Bishnupur, Darbhanga, and Mallik.Statement 1 is incorrect: Dhrupad traces its roots back to the ancient text Sama Veda.Its modern form evolved during the 15th and 16th centuries particularly in the courts of the Rajput kingdoms and the Mughal emperors.The Rajput kings such as Raja Man Singh Tomar of Gwalior were great patrons of Dhrupad.Dhrupad flourished under the patronage of emperors like Akbar, who admired musicians like Tansen.Statement 2 is correct: Dhrupad is deeply rooted in spirituality and devotion.It often incorporates themes from Hindu scriptures, such as the Bhagavad Gita and the Puranas, and is traditionally performed in temples and royal courts.The lyrics of Dhrupad compositions (called prabandhas) are usually in praise of deities like Vishnu
