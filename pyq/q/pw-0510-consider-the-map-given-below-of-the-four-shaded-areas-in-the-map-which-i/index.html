ExplanationThe coastal regions of California(1), particularly cen- tral and southern California, have a textbook Mediterranean climate.Hot dry summers, mild wet winters, and the character- istic chaparral vegetation are all hallmarks of this climate type.Therefore, California is the most accurate representation of a region with a Mediterranean climate.A Mediterranean climate is characterized by :Hot, Dry Summers: Temperatures are warm to hot, with little to no rainfall.Mild, Wet Winters: Winters are cool to mild, with moderate to heavy rainfall.Seasonal Rainfall Pattern: Most of the annual rainfall occurs in the winter months.Clear Skies and Sunshine: Summers are typically sunny with low humidity.Vegetation: Common plants include olive trees, grapevines, shrubs, and evergreen trees, adapted to dry summers.This climate is found in regions like the Mediterranean Ba- sin, California, central Chile, southwestern Australia, and parts of South Africa.UPSC GS Paper-I PYQs 466 495
