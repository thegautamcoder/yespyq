Why are dewdrops not formed on a cloudy night?

aClouds absorb the radiation released from the Earth’s surface.
bClouds reflect back the Earth’s radiation.
cThe Earth’s surface would have low temperatures on cloudy nights.
dClouds deflect the blowing wind to ground level.
✓ Correct answer: b) Clouds reflect back the Earth’s radiation.
ExplanationDew formation occurs when the Earth’s surface cools down sufficiently at night, causing moisture in the air to con- dense into water droplets on cool surfaces.On clear nights, the Earth’s surface loses heat rapidly through a process called radia- tive cooling.This cooling lowers the temperature of surfaces like grass or leaves, leading to the formation of dew as the air reaches its dew point.Option (b) is correct : On cloudy nights, the situation changes.Clouds act like a blanket, reflecting back the infrared radiation (heat) emitted by the Earth’s surface.This process is known as the greenhouse effect at a smaller scale.Because the heat is trapped, the Earth’s surface doesn’t cool enough for dew to form.31
