Consider the map given below: The predominant languages spoken in the areas marked A, B, C and D are respectively:

aBrajbhasha, Bhojpuri, Maithili and Awadhi
bAwadhi, Maithili, Bhojpuri and Brajbhasha
cBrajbhasha, Awadhi, Bhojpuri and Maithili
dMaithili, Bhojpuri, Brajbhasha and Awadhi Geography 441
✓ Correct answer: c) Brajbhasha, Awadhi, Bhojpuri and Maithili
ExplanationA represents Brajbhasha Region: Brajbhasha is predominant- ly spoken in the western districts of Uttar Pradesh, India, in- cluding Mathura, Agra, Aligarh, Etah, Hathras, Bulandshahr, and Badaun.It is characterized by its melodic and gentle tone.Brajbhasha boasts of a rich literary tradition, with renowned po- ets like Surdas, Raskhan, Bihari Lal, and Keshavdas having composed their works in this language.Its influence on Hindi lit- erature is profound particularly in the realm of Krishna Bhakti Kavya (devotional poetry dedicated to Lord Krishna).B represents Awadhi Region: Awadhi is primarily spoken in the central regions of Uttar Pradesh, encompassing districts like Ayodhya, Lucknow, Rae Bareli, Sultanpur, Pratapgarh, Jaunpur, and Faizabad.A distinguishing feature of Awadhi is the prominence of the ‘a’ sound.The epic Ramcharitmanas, a cornerstone of Hindi literature, was composed by Tulsidas in Awadhi.The language’s influence on Hindi literature is substan- tial especially within the Ram Bhakti Kavya tradition (devo- tional poetry dedicated to Lord Rama).C represents Bhojpur
