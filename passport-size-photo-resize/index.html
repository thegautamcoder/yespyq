About the passport-size photo resizer

Measuring 35 × 45 mm, a passport-size photo translates to 413 × 531 px at 300 DPI — the layout that most visa, passport and exam forms expect. Both the pixel size and the file weight have to line up; when they don't match the official spec, the portal refuses the upload, and one rejection can be enough to blow a deadline.

All of it is handled by HTML5 Canvas inside the browser itself, which keeps your personal photos and ID scans on your own device. Nothing is saved to the cloud, nothing shows up in a server log, and there's no privacy cost to using it.

How to get a clean result:
  • Work from a high-resolution original so the finished 413 × 531 px image comes out sharp.
  • Tighten the crop around the face beforehand to hold on to quality.
  • Look over the portal's rules — a fair few insist on both a pixel size and a KB cap.
  • Printing the photo? Use a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the passport size photo resizer free?
Yes, completely free of charge. You won't be asked to register, you won't get a watermark, and there's no limit on how many photos you can put through it.
Is my photo kept private?
Yes, entirely. Because HTML5 Canvas resizes the photo locally in your browser, it's never transmitted to a server and never leaves the device you're on.
Can I use it on a phone?
Definitely. Open it in Chrome or Safari on any iPhone or Android device, bring in your photo, and download the finished file — no installation involved.
Which file formats can I upload?
Upload JPG, JPEG, PNG or WEBP, and HEIC/HEIF too if your browser supports it. Whatever goes in, a JPEG comes out — the safest choice for government and ID portals.
Do I have to install anything?
No installation at all. It runs entirely within your browser — be that Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on desktop as well as mobile.
Is the picture cropped for me?
The crop is yours to set before you process, after which the tool resizes that selected area to the exact pixels and file-size range this format requires.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Yes indeed. An everyday phone photo works fine — grab it from your gallery and the tool adjusts it to meet the official requirements by itself.
