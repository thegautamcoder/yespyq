BMI for Indian competitive exams — what to know

BMI is a quick way to gauge body fat using just your height and weight. Most Indian recruitment exams, though, do not chase a single BMI target — they check that your weight is in proportion to your height. Bodies like the Indian Army, CRPF, BSF and the state police work from height-weight charts calibrated to the Indian population.

If you are targeting NDA: the SSB medical board looks at your height, weight, chest and general fitness together. Being clearly underweight (a BMI under 16) or obese (over 30) will usually get you marked unfit, so aim to sit inside the comfortable 17–25 band that defence hopefuls are expected to hold.

Why the Indian numbers differ: the WHO calls 25–30 overweight and 30+ obese, but Indian medical bodies pull those lines down. The ICMR treats 23 as the start of overweight and 27.5 as obese, since South Asians tend to carry more body fat than Caucasians at an identical BMI.

Writing UPSC, SSC or bank exams? None of these written papers ask for a BMI or any physical standard. A full medical only comes into play later, and only if you are posted to a service with field duties such as the IPS or the IFS forest service.

Keep in mind: because the rules bend with exam, category and year, use this reading as a ballpark and always cross-check the official notification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the BMI requirement for NDA?
There is no hard BMI figure — NDA looks at whether weight matches height. Minimum height is 157.5 cm for the Army and 162.5 cm for Air Force/Navy, and a BMI in the 17–25 range is generally fine for defence aspirants.
What is the formula for BMI?
Divide your weight in kg by your height in metres squared: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²). For 70 kg at 175 cm → 70 ÷ (1.75)² = 70 ÷ 3.0625 = 22.86.
What is a healthy BMI for Indians?
The normal band for Indian adults runs 18.5–22.9. Indian cutoffs are stricter than the WHO's 25/30 lines — the ICMR flags overweight at 23 and obesity at 27.5.
What is the minimum height for NDA?
You need 157.5 cm for the Army Wing and 162.5 cm for the Air Force and Naval Wings, with a 5 cm concession for Gorkha and hill-region candidates.
Does UPSC have a BMI requirement?
The written exam has none. A medical check only happens later for services like the IPS or IFS, where extreme obesity or being underweight can be flagged — but UPSC itself publishes no BMI cutoff.
What BMI is required for police recruitment?
State forces usually skip a BMI figure and instead run a physical efficiency test (running, jumps). Anything in roughly the 18–27 range tends to pass; confirm the details in your state's notification.
