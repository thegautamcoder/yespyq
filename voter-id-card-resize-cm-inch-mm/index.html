About the Voter ID card resizer

A Voter ID (EPIC) card conforms to the global ID-1 card standard of 8.56 × 5.40 cm, and at 300 DPI that comes out to 1011 × 638 px. Lining up the pixels and the file weight together is what counts here, printing most of all: an image that misses the mark prints stretched or blurry, and portals send back anything that doesn't match the stated spec.

Since HTML5 Canvas carries out every step within your browser, your Voter ID image never moves off the device you're working on. Nothing is saved to the cloud, nothing is written to a server log, and your privacy isn't compromised.

Ways to get the sharpest output:
  • Begin with a high-resolution scan so the lettering holds up at 1011 × 638 px.
  • Crop snugly against the card's borders before you resize.
  • Hold the file inside the 20–300 KB band for a tidy, readable result.
  • To print the card, put it through a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Voter ID card resizer free?
Yes, completely free. Nothing to register for, no watermark left behind, and no limit on the number of images you run.
Is my image kept private?
Entirely. HTML5 Canvas processes everything right on your own device, so the image is never transmitted to a server and never leaves your hands.
Can I use it on a phone?
You bet. Pull it up in Chrome or Safari on any Android or iPhone, feed in your image and download what comes out — no app to install.
Which file formats can I upload?
Upload JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP or HEIC/HEIF wherever the browser supports it. The result is always JPEG, the format government and ID portals accept most broadly.
Do I have to install anything?
Not at all. It operates entirely within the browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on desktop or mobile.
Is the image cropped for me?
You set the crop yourself beforehand, and the tool then resizes that region to the precise pixel size and weight range this format requires.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Absolutely. An ordinary phone photo works fine — select it from your gallery and the tool matches it to the required dimensions automatically.
