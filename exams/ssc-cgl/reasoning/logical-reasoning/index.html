Q1
Consider the given statement/s to be true and decide which of the given conclusions/assumptions can definitely be drawn from the given statement. Statement: Laugh and the world will laugh with you. Assumptions: I. People generally laugh. II. Laughter symbolises happiness.
aOnly assumption I is implicit
bOnly assumption II is implicit
cBoth I and II assumptions are implicit
dNeither I nor II assumption is implicit
✓ Correct answer: b) Only assumption II is implicit
ExplanationStatement: Laugh and the world will laugh with you.This suggests that laughter is contagious or that people tend to respond to laughter positively.Evaluating the Assumptions: Assumption I: "People generally laugh." The statement does not indicate whether people generally laugh.It only suggests that if you laugh, others might laugh with you.This assumption is not necessarily true based on the given statement.Assumption II: "Laughter symbolizes happiness." The statement implies that laughter is a positive reaction, which is often associated with happiness.This assumption is reasonable and valid because laughter is generally seen as a sign of joy.Hence, Only assumption II is implicit is the correct answer.
Q2
Consider the given statement/s to be true and decide which of the given conclusions/assumptions can definitely be drawn from the given statement. Statement: If he works hard he will be successful in life. Conclusions: I. He is a hard worker. Conclusions: II. Hard work pays
aBoth I and II follow
bOnly I follows
cOnly II follows
dNeither I nor II follow
✓ Correct answer: c) Only II follows
ExplanationLet's analyze carefully.Statement: If he works hard, he will be successful in life.This is a conditional statement — it tells us what will happen if he works hard.It does not say whether he actually works hard or not.Conclusion I: He is a hard worker.This does not follow, because the statement never says he does work hard — only what would happen if he did.So, ❌ Conclusion I does not follow.Conclusion II: Hard work pays.This is consistent with the statement — the statement implies that working hard leads to success, i.e., hard work pays.So, ✅ Conclusion II follows.Hence, Only II follows is the correct answer.

cNeither inference I nor II is true.
dOnly inference I is true.
✓ Correct answer: c) Neither inference I nor II is true.
ExplanationStatement: "Fuel prices in India are inflating day by day, and there is no upper limit in its price increase." This statement simply mentions that fuel prices are continuously rising and there is no maximum limit on how high they could go.Inferences: I. Central and State governments have not cut down their respective taxes on fuel.The statement does not directly mention anything about taxes imposed by the government.It only focuses on the inflation in fuel prices without providing specific details about tax policies.Hence, we cannot infer this directly from the given statement.II.Inflation in the international market is influencing the rising fuel prices.This inference is plausible, as fuel prices in many countries are influenced by global factors such as oil prices in the international market.However, the statement does not explicitly mention international market conditions as a factor.While it may be reasonable to assume that international inflation is affecting prices, it is not stated directly, so we cannot conclusively say this is true based on the given statement alone.Conclusion: Since neither inference is explicitly supported by the statement, the correct answer would be: C. Neither inference I nor II is true.Hence, option(C) is the correct answer.
