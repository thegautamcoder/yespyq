Q3
Sea water is saltier than rain water because
aSea animals are salt producing
bThe air around the sea is saltish
cRivers wash away salts from earth and pour them into the sea
dSea beds have salt producing mines
✓ Correct answer: c) Rivers wash away salts from earth and pour them into the sea
ExplanationRainwater is pure but when it flows as rivers, it dissolves salts and minerals from rocks and soil.These dissolved salts are carried by rivers into the sea continuously over millions of years.Since water evaporates from the sea but salt does not, the salt concentration keeps increasing.Hence, the correct answer is "Rivers wash away salts from earth and pour them into the sea."

Q47
Polar easterlies are which kind of winds?
aSeasonal winds
bPermanent winds
cSea storm
dLocal winds
✓ Correct answer: b) Permanent winds
ExplanationPolar easterlies are permanent winds that blow from the east in the high latitudes of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.They are caused by the difference in air pressure between the polar highs and the subpolar lows.The polar highs are areas of high pressure that are located around the poles.The subpolar lows are areas of low pressure that are located in the middle latitudes.The difference in air pressure causes the air to flow from the polar highs to the subpolar lows.Hence, the correct answer is "Permanent winds".

Q50
As the river enters the plain it twists and turns forming large bends known as ______.

blevees
cfloodplains
dmeanders
✓ Correct answer: d) meanders
ExplanationThe construction of a complete S curve known as Meanders may result from the formation of sequential bends that are arranged in reverse order.In a river reach, a river is said to be meandering when it develops a series of reverse-order loops joined by brief straight stretches known as crossings.A river's tendency to meander is caused by the erodibility of the streambed and banks.Overabundance of river sediment causes more turbulence during floods.When the amount of silt charge exceeds what is needed for stability, the river begins to build up its slope by depositing silt into its bed.The channel's breadth tends to rise as the bed slope increases if the banks don't resist.When river water attacks the banks, it's possible that one bank will be attacked slightly more than the other, which will result in a tiny flow divergence.More and more flow is improved toward one bank than the other because to this little divergence from the regular axial flow.One bank becomes convex and another concave as a result of this ongoing process.As a result, meandering can also be caused by rising slopes.Hence, the correct answer is ''meanders''.
