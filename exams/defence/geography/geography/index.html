cMetamorphic
dExtrusive Igneous
✓ Correct answer: a) Igneous
ExplanationIgneous rock or magmatic rock is made up of molten magma.It is one of the three main rock types; sedimentary and metamorphic.When magma cools and solidifies, it forms igneous rocks.These rocks can be classified into two main types: intrusive (or plutonic) igneous rocks, which form below the Earth's surface, and extrusive (or volcanic) igneous rocks, which form on the surface.Both types originate from the cooling and crystallization of molten magma.Examples include granite (intrusive) and basalt (extrusive).Hence, the correct answer is option (A).

Q34
River Delta Formation is a result of which process?
aErosion
bEvaporation
cSedimentation
dDeposition
✓ Correct answer: d) Deposition
ExplanationErosion: Erosion is the process of wearing away rocks and soil by the action of wind, water, or ice.While erosion plays a role in the transportation of sediment, it is not the primary process responsible for the formation of river deltas.Evaporation: Evaporation is the process by which water changes from a liquid to a gas or vapor.It is a part of the water cycle but is not directly related to the formation of river deltas.Sedimentation: Sedimentation is the process of sediment settling out of a liquid and onto a surface.In the case of river deltas, sedimentation is closely related to deposition.As the river's velocity decreases upon entering a larger body of water, sediment carried by the river is deposited, leading to the formation of a delta.Deposition: When a river flows into a larger body of water such as an ocean or a lake, the velocity of the river decreases, leading to the deposition of sediment.Over time, this build-up of sediment forms a river delta.Hence, the correct answer is Option D i.e. Deposition.
