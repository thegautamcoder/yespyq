Q2
The process of translation is
aribosome synthesis
bprotein synthesis
cDNA synthesis
dRNA synthesis
✓ Correct answer: b) protein synthesis
ExplanationTranslation is the process where the information in messenger RNA (mRNA) is used to synthesize proteins.This process occurs in the ribosome, where transfer RNA (tRNA) reads the mRNA code and assembles amino acids in the correct sequence to form a protein.
Q3

aNon-histonic double stranded DNA
bHistonic double stranded DNA
cHistone & DNA both are absent
dHistone without DNA
✓ Correct answer: a) Non-histonic double stranded DNA
ExplanationThe genetic material of a prokaryotic cell is: Option A: Non-histonic double stranded DNA Explanation: Prokaryotic cells contain double-stranded DNA that is not associated with histones.Unlike eukaryotic cells, prokaryotic DNA is circular and lacks histone proteins, which are present in eukaryotic chromosomes to help in DNA packaging.

Q8

aGenome, chromosome, nucleotide, gene
bGenome, chromosome, gene, nucleotide
cChromosome, genome, nucleotide, gene
dChromosome, gene, genome, nucleotide
✓ Correct answer: b) Genome, chromosome, gene, nucleotide
ExplanationThe genome is described as the genetic information repository of an organism.The genetic material, i.e., DNA is packaged into thread-like structures called chromosomes.Gene is the segment of DNA that codes for the protein.Nucleotide is the building block of genetic material.Therefore, the correct order of organisation is: Genome - Chromosome - Gene - Nucleotide [New NCERT Class 12th Page No. 83]
Q9
Taylor conducted the experiments to prove semiconservative mode of chromosome replication on;
aVinca rosea.
bVicia faba.
cDrosophila melanogaster.
dE. coli.
✓ Correct answer: b) Vicia faba.
ExplanationTaylor and his colleagues conducted experiments on root tip cells of Vicia faba (broad bean) to demonstrate the semiconservative mode of chromosome replication in eukaryotes.They used radioactive thymidine (^3H-thymidine) to label newly synthesized DNA and tracked it through successive cell divisions using autoradiography.The results showed that after one replication cycle, only one chromatid was labeled, and after two cycles, only half the chromatids were labeled — supporting semiconservative replication, where each new DNA molecule consists of one parental and one newly synthesized strand.In contrast, E. coli was used in Meselson and Stahl’s famous DNA replication experiment.
Q10
A molecule that can act as a genetic material must fulfill the traits given below, except;
ait should be able to express itself in the form of Mendelian characters.
bit should be able to generate its replica.
cit should be unstable structurally and chemically.
dit should provide the scope for slow changes that are required for evolution.
✓ Correct answer: c) it should be unstable structurally and chemically.
ExplanationA genetic material must be structurally and chemically stable to preserve the integrity of genetic information across generations.It must also be capable of replication, expression (producing traits), and allowing slow, heritable variations for evolution.DNA fulfills all these criteria.An unstable molecule would degrade or mutate rapidly, disrupting genetic continuity.Therefore, instability is not a desirable trait for genetic material, making option C the correct answer as the exception among the listed traits.

Q17
Given below are two statements: Statement I : In prokaryotes, the positively charged DNA is held with some negatively charged proteins in a region called nucleoid. Statement II : In eukaryotes, the negatively charged DNA is wrapped around the positively charged histone octamer to form nucleosome. In the light of the above statements, choose the correct answer from the options given below : [NEET 2023]
aBoth Statement I and Statement II are false.
bStatement I is correct but Statement II is false.
cStatement I incorrect but Statement II is true.
dBoth Statement I and Statement II are true.
✓ Correct answer: c) Statement I incorrect but Statement II is true.
ExplanationIn prokaryotes, such as, E. coli, though they do not have a defined nucleus, the DNA is not scattered throughout the cell.DNA (being negatively charged) is held with some proteins (that have positive charges) in a region termed as ‘nucleoid’.The DNA in nucleoid is organised in large loops held by proteins.In eukaryotes, this organisation is much more complex.There is a set of positively charged, basic proteins called histones.A protein acquires charge depending upon the abundance of amino acids residues with charged side chains.Histones are rich in the basic amino acid residues lysine and arginine.Both the amino acid residues carry positive charges in their side chains.Histones are organised to form a unit of eight molecules called histone octamer.The negatively charged DNA is wrapped around the positively charged histone octamer to form a structure called nucleosome.

Q23
A transcription unit in DNA is defined primarily by the three regions in DNA and these are with respect to upstream and down stream end; [NEET 2024]
aPromotor, Structural gene, Terminator
bRepressor, Operator gene, Structural gene
cStructural gene, Transposons, Operator gene
dInducer, Repressor, Structural gene
✓ Correct answer: a) Promotor, Structural gene, Terminator
ExplanationA transcription unit in DNA consists of three primary regions:1. Promoter: The region where RNA polymerase binds to initiate transcription.2. Structural gene: The region that codes for the RNA molecule to be transcribed.3. Terminator: The region that signals the end of transcription.These elements define the transcription unit and help regulate the process of transcription in both prokaryotes and eukaryotes.
