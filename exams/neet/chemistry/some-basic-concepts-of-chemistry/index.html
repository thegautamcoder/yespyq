Q1
The number of molecules and moles in 2.8375 litres of O2 at STP are respectively
a7.527 × 1022 and 0.250 mol
b1.505 × 1023 and 0.250 mol
c7.527 × 1023 and 0.125 mol
d7.527 × 1022 and 0.125 mol
✓ Correct answer: d) 7.527 × 1022 and 0.125 mol
ExplanationGiven: Mass = 0.57 g, Volume = 100 mL = 0.1 L At STP, molar volume = 22.4 L Moles = 0.1 / 22.4 = 0.004464 mol Molar mass = 0.57 / 0.004464 ≈ 127.7 g/mol Let metal chloride be MClₓ %Cl = (35.5x / (M + 35.5x)) × 100 = 55 Putting M + 35.5x = 127.7, solve: 35.5x = 70.24 ⇒ x = 2 So formula = MCl₂ Answer: Option A
Q2
A metal chloride contains 55.0% of chlorine by weight. 100 mL vapours of the metal chloride at STP weigh 0.57 g. The molecular formula of the metal chloride is (Given: Atomic mass of chlorine is 35.5 u)
aMCl2
bMCl4
cMCl3
dMCl
✓ Correct answer: a) MCl2
ExplanationGiven: Mass = 0.57 g, Volume = 100 mL = 0.1 L At STP, molar volume = 22.4 L Moles = 0.1 / 22.4 = 0.004464 mol Molar mass = 0.57 / 0.004464 ≈ 127.7 g/mol Let metal chloride be MClₓ %Cl = (35.5x / (M + 35.5x)) × 100 = 55 Putting M + 35.5x = 127.7, solve: 35.5x = 70.24 ⇒ x = 2 So formula = MCl₂ Answer: Option A
Q3
When a hydrocarbon A undergoes complete combustion it requires 11 equivalents of oxygen and produces 4 equivalents of water. What is the molecular formula of A?
aC9H8
bC11H4
cC5H8
dC11H8
✓ Correct answer: a) C9H8
ExplanationCorrect answer is A. Dalton’s atomic theory states that compounds form when atoms combine in a fixed simple whole number ratio, not any ratio.So option A is incorrect.Option B matches Dalton’s postulate, though it is now known isotopes exist.Option C is Dalton’s idea; although atoms are divisible (electrons, protons exist), this was Dalton’s belief.Option D is also correct per Dalton—he proposed that chemical reactions are rearrangements of atoms.Thus, option A contradicts Dalton’s theory and is the only incorrect statement among the given choices.
Q4
A sample of CaCO₃ and MgCO3 weighed 2.21 g is ignited to constant weight of 1.152 g. The composition of mixture is: (Given molar mass in g mol–1 CaCO3 : 100, MgCO3 : 84)
a1.187 g CaCO3 + 1.023 g MgCO3
b1.023 g CaCO3 + 1.023 g MgCO3
c1.187 g CaCO3 + 1.187 g MgCO3
d1.023 g CaCO3 + 1.187 g MgCO3
✓ Correct answer: a) 1.187 g CaCO3 + 1.023 g MgCO3
ExplanationGiven: C = 42.1%, H = 6.4%, O = 100 − 48.5 = 51.5% Divide by atomic masses: C → 42.1 ÷ 12 = 3.508 H → 6.4 ÷ 1 = 6.4 O → 51.5 ÷ 16 = 3.219 Divide all by 3.219: C ≈ 1.09, H ≈ 1.99, O ≈ 1 Empirical formula ≈ C₁H₂O₁ Empirical mass = 12 + 2 + 16 = 30 n = 342 ÷ 30 = 11.4 ≈ 11 Molecular formula = C₁₁H₂₂O₁₁ Answer: Option D

Q6
An organic compound has 42.1% carbon, 6.4% hydrogen and remainder is oxygen. If its molecular weight is 342, then its molecular formula is:
aC11H18O12
bC12H20O12
cC14H20O10
dC12H22O11
✓ Correct answer: d) C12H22O11
Explanation(a) All atoms of a specific element are identical in mass, size, and other properties.However, atoms of different elements exhibit different properties and vary in mass and size.This statement is correct.(b) Matter consists of divisible atoms.(This is incorrect because atoms are indivisible, according to Dalton's atomic theory.) (d) All the atoms of a given element have different properties including mass.(This is incorrect because atoms of the same element have the same properties, according to Dalton's atomic theory.)
