About the PAN card photo resizer

When you apply for a PAN card, the form wants a passport-style headshot squeezed into a narrow file budget: 213 × 213 px, sitting somewhere between 10 and 50 KB. You have to satisfy the pixel measurement and the file weight together — miss either and the portal rejects the picture, which can stall the whole application.

The conversion runs on HTML5 Canvas straight in the browser tab, so your snapshots and scanned documents never leave the device you're using. Nothing is stored in the cloud, nothing lands in a server log, and your privacy stays intact.

How to get a clean result:
  • Pick a bright, sharply focused original so it still looks good once shrunk.
  • Cut the frame down to the face first — quality holds up better that way.
  • Keep an eye on the KB ceiling; PAN forms are unforgiving about file weight.
  • If you're printing it, run it through a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the PAN card photo resizer free?
Yes — free through and through. No account to create, no watermark stamped on, and no ceiling on how many pictures you resize.
Is my photo kept private?
Totally. HTML5 Canvas does the resizing on your own machine, so the image is never uploaded to any server and doesn't leave your device.
Can I use it on a phone?
Yes. Just open it in Chrome or Safari on an Android phone or iPhone, load your photo, and download what comes out — no app needed.
Which file formats can I upload?
It takes JPG, JPEG, PNG and WEBP, plus HEIC/HEIF when your browser handles them. It hands back a JPEG every time, because that's the format government and ID portals accept most reliably.
Do I have to install anything?
Nope. The whole thing runs inside your browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on either a desktop or a phone.
Is the picture cropped for me?
You handle the crop yourself before processing, and then the tool resizes that selection to the exact pixels and file-size range this format demands.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Yes. A regular photo from your phone is fine — select it from the gallery and the tool matches it to the official spec for you.
