Age is fixed as on the first day of the month the course begins — not the day you fill the form or write the exam. CDS runs twice a year (CDS I and CDS II), and candidates must hold or be finishing a Bachelor's degree — B.E./B.Tech for INA, and either a Bachelor's degree or B.E./B.Tech with Physics and Maths at Class 12 for AFA.

How CDS age eligibility works

Unlike NDA, CDS candidates are graduates or final-year students, so every academy's window sits several years higher. Each academy also runs its own band — the widest is OTA at 19–25, the narrowest is INA at 19–22.

The date that counts is the first day of the course commencement month, not the day you fill the form or write the exam. Your age on that fixed cut-off decides eligibility, academy by academy.

Note: age bars and course-specific rules can shift with each notification. Use this calculator for a quick check, then confirm your eligibility against the official CDS notification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the CDS age limit?
It differs by academy: IMA is 19–24 years, INA is 19–22 years, AFA is 19–23 years, and OTA is 19–25 years. Unlike NDA, CDS candidates have already graduated, so the windows sit higher.
What is the age limit for IMA?
19 to 24 years as on the first day of the course you're applying for.
What is the age limit for OTA?
19 to 25 years — the widest window among the four CDS academies, and the only one open to both men and women.
What is the age limit for INA?
19 to 22 years — the narrowest CDS window, reflecting the Naval Academy's B.Tech course structure.
What is the age limit for AFA?
19 to 23 years for the Air Force Academy's flying branch.
Can women apply for CDS?
Yes, through OTA for the Non-Technical (Women) entry. IMA, INA and AFA remain open to unmarried male candidates only.
What is the CDS photo size requirement?
A recent colour photo of around 400×400 pixels, 20 to 300 KB, in JPG. Confirm the exact specifications in the current notification.
