Frequently asked questions

Does resizing in cm, inch or mm cost anything?
It is free through and through — no account to open, no watermark left behind, and no cap on how many images you run.
Is my image kept private?
Fully. The resize is carried out by HTML5 Canvas inside your own browser, so the image is never handed to a server and never leaves your device.
Can I use it on a phone?
Yes indeed. Open YESPYQ in Chrome or Safari on any iPhone or Android, add your image, and download what comes out — there is no app to fetch.
Which image formats can I upload?
Bring JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP or HEIC/HEIF (as far as your browser supports it). The result is always JPEG, which pairs best with Indian government exam and ID portals.
Is any software installation required?
None whatsoever. It all takes place in the browser you already use — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on desktop or mobile.
Which DPI is right for printing?
Lean on 300 DPI for routine prints such as ID cards, exam photos and documents: 1 inch = 300 px, 1 cm ≈ 118 px, 1 mm ≈ 11.8 px. The tool measures in exact pixels, so plug in the pixel numbers these give.
Why does it save as JPEG rather than PNG?
Almost all Indian government exam and ID portals ask for JPEG, and it keeps files much lighter than PNG while holding photo quality — exactly what online form uploads call for.
