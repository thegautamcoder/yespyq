High-yield Economy topics in UPSC PYQs

Across past papers, the same Economy themes resurface repeatedly. These are the areas to prioritise when you practise previous year questions:

1. Money, banking & monetary policy

RBI tools (repo, CRR, SLR, OMO), types of money, and the banking structure are tested almost every year.

2. Fiscal policy, budget & taxation

Deficits, the budget process, GST and direct/indirect taxes form a dense, conceptual PYQ zone.

3. Growth, inflation & national income

GDP/GNP concepts, inflation measures (CPI/WPI) and indices recur in definition-checking questions.

4. External sector

Balance of payments, exchange rates, FDI/FPI and trade terms connect strongly to current affairs.

5. Planning, schemes & institutions

Government schemes, financial-sector regulators and economic institutions appear regularly.

Economy PYQs test definitions precisely — be sure you can distinguish look-alike terms like fiscal vs revenue deficit, or CRR vs SLR.

How to use Economy PYQs effectively

  • Solve theme-wise, not just year-wise. Group questions by topic to see the repetition that PYQs reveal.
  • Read every explanation. The reasoning behind a wrong option is often the next year's question.
  • Revisit, don't just attempt. Re-solve Economy PYQs in timed sets to lock in accuracy and speed.
  • Link to the syllabus. Use PYQs as a map of what to study deeply versus what to skim.

Frequently asked questions

Is Economy hard to score in UPSC Prelims?

Economy is conceptual and current-affairs-linked, but PYQs show a clear pattern of repeated terms that make it very learnable.

How many Economy questions appear in Prelims?

Economy typically accounts for 13–20 questions, often overlapping with current affairs and government schemes.

Do I need to read the Economic Survey for PYQs?

Read it selectively. PYQs show which themes matter; pair them with the Survey's key chapters rather than reading cover to cover.
