High-yield Geography topics in UPSC PYQs

Across past papers, the same Geography themes resurface repeatedly. These are the areas to prioritise when you practise previous year questions:

1. Physical geography

Geomorphology, climatology (monsoon, cyclones), oceanography and the atmosphere are conceptual cores that repeat in varied forms.

2. Indian geography

Drainage systems, soils, vegetation, agriculture and mineral/industrial distribution are heavily tested, often map-linked.

3. World geography

Major landforms, climatic regions, ocean currents and important straits/locations appear regularly.

4. Economic & human geography

Resources, energy, transport and population distribution connect Geography to current affairs.

5. Map & location-based questions

Rivers, mountain passes, tiger reserves and neighbouring-country borders demand atlas familiarity.

Keep an atlas open while solving Geography PYQs — half the marks here come from knowing exactly where things are.

How to use Geography PYQs effectively

  • Solve theme-wise, not just year-wise. Group questions by topic to see the repetition that PYQs reveal.
  • Read every explanation. The reasoning behind a wrong option is often the next year's question.
  • Revisit, don't just attempt. Re-solve Geography PYQs in timed sets to lock in accuracy and speed.
  • Link to the syllabus. Use PYQs as a map of what to study deeply versus what to skim.

Frequently asked questions

Is Geography scoring for UPSC Prelims?

Yes, the physical-geography portion is conceptual and stable, so it rewards consistent PYQ practice and map work.

How many Geography questions come in Prelims?

Geography generally contributes 10–15 questions, and overlaps with Environment and current affairs.

How do I prepare map-based Geography PYQs?

Solve location questions with an atlas beside you and revise a map of India's rivers, ranges and reserves repeatedly.
