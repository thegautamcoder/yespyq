High-yield History topics in UPSC PYQs

Across past papers, the same History themes resurface repeatedly. These are the areas to prioritise when you practise previous year questions:

1. Modern India & the freedom struggle

The highest-yield zone: phases of the national movement, key sessions of the Congress, acts and reforms, and the contributions of leaders.

2. Art & Culture

Temple architecture, classical dances, painting schools, music traditions and UNESCO heritage sites are tested with growing depth.

3. Ancient India

The Indus Valley Civilisation, Vedic society, Mauryan and Gupta administration, and Buddhist/Jain traditions recur frequently.

4. Medieval India

Delhi Sultanate and Mughal administration, the Bhakti and Sufi movements, and regional kingdoms feature regularly.

5. Personalities & institutions

Reformers, revolutionaries and the institutions they founded are a favourite matching-and-statement format.

Art & Culture questions reward visual familiarity — link each temple, dance and painting style to its region and dynasty.

How to use History PYQs effectively

  • Solve theme-wise, not just year-wise. Group questions by topic to see the repetition that PYQs reveal.
  • Read every explanation. The reasoning behind a wrong option is often the next year's question.
  • Revisit, don't just attempt. Re-solve History PYQs in timed sets to lock in accuracy and speed.
  • Link to the syllabus. Use PYQs as a map of what to study deeply versus what to skim.

Frequently asked questions

Which part of History is most important for UPSC Prelims?

Modern India and the freedom struggle carry the most weight, followed by Art & Culture, which has grown sharply in recent years.

How many History questions appear in UPSC Prelims?

History & Art-Culture together usually contribute 12–18 questions in the Prelims GS Paper-I.

Are NCERTs enough for History PYQs?

NCERTs build the base, but solving PYQs alongside them shows where extra depth in Art & Culture and Modern History is needed.
