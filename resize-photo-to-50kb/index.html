Why resize a photo to 50 KB?

When you go to attach a candidate photograph, portals like UPSC, SSC, IBPS, SBI and NTA NEET will only take one that sits inside a set KB limit — commonly around 50 KB. If your photo runs even a touch over that ceiling, the upload form turns it away, so the file has to be slimmed down before the application will submit.

Doing it the traditional way through Paint, Photoshop or GIMP means installing software and clicking through several manual steps. This tool works right in the browser and reaches 50 KB in one tap — nothing gets uploaded, there is no account to open and it costs nothing.

Tips for the best result

  • Start with a sharp, well-lit passport-style shot where the face is clearly visible against a plain white or light backdrop.
  • Trim in tight around the face with just a little margin before resizing — the closer the crop, the more detail the tool can preserve.
  • Feed in the sharpest original you have on hand; the tool then compresses cleverly to reach the 50 KB mark.
  • If the 50 KB photo comes out a little soft, supply a bigger original and run it through once more.
Good to know: your photo always comes back as a JPEG — the exact format Indian exam and ID portals look for — so it goes through the upload on the first try.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Resize Photo to 50 KB tool free?
It is, with nothing held back. You never sign up, the finished photo carries no watermark, and you can run as many photos through it as you want.
Is my photo kept private?
Entirely. The resizing happens inside your own browser through HTML5 Canvas, so the photo is never uploaded anywhere and stays put on your device.
Does it work on a phone?
It does. Just open this page in Chrome or Safari on any Android phone or iPhone, add your photo and save the result — there is no app to download.
Which file formats can I use?
You can feed in JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP, or HEIC/HEIF where the browser allows it. The photo comes back as a JPEG, the format Indian exam and ID portals expect.
Do I need to install anything?
Nothing at all. It all happens inside the browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — whether you are on a laptop or a phone.
Will resizing to 50 KB lower the quality?
The resizer holds on to as much sharpness as possible while landing at 50 KB. Since 50 KB leaves generous room, the photo normally comes out clear and clean.
Why do exam portals set a KB file-size limit?
Boards such as SSC, UPSC, IBPS, NTA and RRB set KB caps so their databases stay lean and uploads finish fast even on weak connections. Cross the limit and the form simply turns the photo away.
