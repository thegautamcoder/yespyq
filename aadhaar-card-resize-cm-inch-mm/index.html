About the Aadhaar card resizer

The Aadhaar card sticks to the worldwide ID-1 format — 8.56 × 5.40 cm — which translates to 1011 × 638 px once you set a print-ready 300 DPI. Nailing the pixels and the file weight together really counts when you're printing: an off-spec image ends up stretched or fuzzy, and portals turn back anything that doesn't fit the published requirements.

Because the work is handled in your browser by HTML5 Canvas, your Aadhaar image stays put on the device you're using. Nothing gets parked in the cloud, nothing lands in a server log, and there's no privacy cost.

Pointers for a sharp result:
  • Work from a high-resolution scan so the text stays legible at 1011 × 638 px.
  • Trim right up to the card's edges before you resize.
  • Keep the output inside the 20–300 KB band to stay crisp and readable.
  • When printing the card, run it through a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Aadhaar card resizer free?
It is, top to bottom. No account to create, no watermark stamped on, and no ceiling on how many images you push through.
Is my image kept private?
Fully. HTML5 Canvas does the work right on your own machine, so the image is never shipped to a server and never leaves the device you're on.
Can I use it on a phone?
Sure. Load it in Chrome or Safari on any Android or iPhone, drop in your image and save the result — no app needed.
Which file formats can I upload?
Bring JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP or HEIC/HEIF where the browser allows it. It always writes JPEG on the way out, since that's what government and ID portals handle most dependably.
Do I have to install anything?
Nope. The whole thing lives in your browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on a desktop or a phone alike.
Is the image cropped for me?
You handle the crop yourself first, and from there the tool sizes that region to the exact pixel dimensions and weight band this format expects.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Yes indeed. A plain phone photo is plenty — grab it from your gallery and the tool fits it to the required dimensions automatically.
