Frequently asked questions

What is the UPSC CSE photo size in pixels?
For most cycles the form expects a square 400×400 pixel image on a white background, saved as JPG and weighing between 20 and 300 KB. Always confirm against the year's notification, since UPSC can revise it.
What is the UPSC signature size?
Upload a scanned signature at 400×400 pixels, 20 to 100 KB, in JPG. Write it with a black pen on clean white paper, then scan at 200 DPI or higher for a crisp result.
Can I use a mobile photo for the UPSC CSE application?
A phone camera is perfectly fine as long as the shot ticks every box — plain white backdrop, even light, the whole face in frame, then trimmed to 400×400 px and kept under 300 KB as a JPG. The resizer handles that last part.
What happens if I upload a wrong-sized photo for UPSC?
Either the portal refuses the file outright, or it slips through and gets queried later when your application is scrutinised. Matching the stated size upfront closes off both problems.
Does UPSC accept PNG photos?
No — the online form is built for JPG/JPEG. If your image is a PNG, save it as a JPG first and then upload.
How recent must the UPSC photo be?
Use a fresh photograph, ideally one clicked no more than three months before you fill in the form.
