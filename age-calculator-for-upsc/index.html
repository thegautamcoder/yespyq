How UPSC CSE age eligibility works

UPSC applies the age bar to the letter. Beyond the category relaxations listed above, there is no room for exceptions — fall outside the window by a single day and you are out for that cycle.

The date that matters is 1 August of the exam year. Not the day you fill the form, not the day you write the paper — your age on that fixed cut-off is the only thing that settles eligibility.

Note: the age bars and attempt caps may be revised with each notification. Use this calculator for a quick check, then confirm your eligibility against the official UPSC CSE notification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the UPSC CSE age limit for the general category?
General-category candidates must be between 21 and 32 years as on 1 August of the exam year — old enough to have hit 21 and not yet past 32 on that date.
How many attempts are allowed in UPSC CSE?
The caps run: 6 for general, 9 for OBC (non-creamy layer), and no ceiling for SC/ST up to the age limit. PwBD candidates from General/OBC get 9, while PwBD from SC/ST have unlimited tries.
What is the cut-off date for age calculation?
Your age is taken as on 1 August of the exam year — so for UPSC CSE 2026 it is worked out as on 1 August 2026.
What qualification do I need?
Any bachelor's degree from a recognised university will do. Those still in their final year can apply, provided they furnish proof of passing at the interview / DAF stage.
Can I apply at 21?
Yes. Twenty-one is the floor, measured on 1 August of the exam year, so reaching 21 on or before that date makes you eligible.
Is category-based relaxation automatic?
It is not. You have to state your category on the form and produce a valid certificate at verification — skip that and no relaxation is applied.
Is the UPSC NDA age limit the same?
No, NDA runs on a different clock — roughly 16.5 to 19.5 years on the first day of the course month — which has nothing to do with the CSE limits.
