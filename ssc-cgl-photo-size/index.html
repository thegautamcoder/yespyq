Frequently asked questions

What is the SSC CGL photo size?
The photograph should measure 275×354 pixels and sit between 20 KB and 50 KB, saved in JPG against a plain white background.
What is the SSC CGL signature size?
Your signature goes in at 236×79 pixels and 10–20 KB as a JPG. Sign on white paper using black or blue ink.
Why is the SSC CGL photo limit so small (up to 50 KB)?
Tight KB caps let government servers store millions of files cheaply and let candidates on weak internet upload without timing out. The resizer squeezes any image below 50 KB while keeping your face clear enough for verification.
Does the SSC CGL photo need a specific ratio?
It does. The 275×354 pixel spec works out to about a 3:4 passport shape, so a square or wide photo can be turned away at upload.
Can I use a smartphone camera for the SSC CGL photo?
Absolutely — just make sure the background is plain white, the lighting is good, your full face shows, and the file ends up at 275×354 px and 20–50 KB in JPG.
What happens if I upload an oversized photo for SSC CGL?
The site throws an error and blocks the upload. Bring the image back within the stated pixels and KB range, then try the upload once more.
