Gini Coefficient and Lorenz Curve Explained

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

The Gini coefficient is a widely used measure of income or wealth inequality within a population, derived from the Lorenz curve.

How it works

The Gini coefficient ranges from 0 to 1: a value of 0 means perfect equality (everyone has the same income) and 1 means perfect inequality (one person has all the income).

The Lorenz curve

The Lorenz curve plots the cumulative share of income against the cumulative share of the population; the further it bows away from the line of equality, the greater the inequality.

Why it matters for UPSC

The Gini coefficient's 0–1 scale and its link to the Lorenz curve are commonly tested inequality concepts.
