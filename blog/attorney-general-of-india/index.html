Attorney General of India Explained

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

The Attorney General of India is the highest law officer of the Union, provided for under Article 76 of the Constitution.

Role

The Attorney General advises the Government of India on legal matters and represents it in the Supreme Court and High Courts. They have the right of audience in all courts and can take part in parliamentary proceedings without a right to vote.

Appointment

Appointed by the President, the Attorney General holds office during the pleasure of the President and must be qualified to be a Supreme Court judge.

Why it matters for UPSC

Article 76, the AG's role and their status are common Polity Prelims facts.
