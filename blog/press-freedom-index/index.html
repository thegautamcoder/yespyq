The World Press Freedom Index is an annual ranking of countries by the degree of freedom available to journalists and media. It is published by Reporters Without Borders (RSF).

What it assesses

The index evaluates media independence, pluralism, the legislative environment, transparency and the safety of journalists.

Debate

Governments sometimes contest the methodology, but the index is widely cited in discussions on media freedom and democracy.

Why it matters for UPSC

The publisher (Reporters Without Borders) and the index's link to democracy and rights make it a recurring current-affairs point.
