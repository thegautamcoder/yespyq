National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020)

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

The National Education Policy 2020 (NEP) is India's first comprehensive education policy in over three decades, replacing the 1986 policy. It aims to transform schooling and higher education by 2040, with a focus on flexibility, foundational learning and Indian languages.

The 5+3+3+4 school structure

NEP replaces the old 10+2 structure with a 5+3+3+4 design — Foundational (ages 3–8), Preparatory (8–11), Middle (11–14) and Secondary (14–18) stages, bringing early childhood care and education into the formal system.

Language and foundational literacy

The policy stresses mother tongue or regional language as the medium of instruction at least until Grade 5, and sets universal foundational literacy and numeracy as an urgent national mission.

Higher education reforms

NEP proposes a multidisciplinary approach, a single higher-education regulator (HECI), multiple entry–exit options with an Academic Bank of Credits, and a target of raising the Gross Enrolment Ratio in higher education to 50% by 2035.

Why it matters for UPSC

NEP 2020 is heavily tested in both Prelims (factual features like the 5+3+3+4 structure, GER targets) and Mains (governance, education reform). It also recurs in current affairs whenever implementation steps are announced.
