Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY)

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Ayushman Bharat is India's flagship health initiative, designed to move from selective to universal, comprehensive health coverage. It has two interconnected components.

Two pillars

The first pillar is Health and Wellness Centres (now Ayushman Arogya Mandirs) delivering comprehensive primary care. The second is Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY), a health-assurance scheme.

PM-JAY cover

PM-JAY provides health cover of up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation to poor and vulnerable families identified through the Socio-Economic Caste Census, making it one of the largest such schemes in the world.

Cashless and portable

Beneficiaries get cashless, paperless treatment at empanelled public and private hospitals, and coverage is portable across states.

Why it matters for UPSC

PM-JAY details — the ₹5 lakh cover, the two pillars, and beneficiary identification — are classic Prelims facts and useful Mains examples on health and welfare.

Frequently asked questions

What health cover does PM-JAY provide?

Up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation.

What are the two components of Ayushman Bharat?

Health and Wellness Centres (Ayushman Arogya Mandirs) for primary care, and PM-JAY for hospitalisation cover.
