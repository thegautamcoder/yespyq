Disinvestment is the process by which the government sells or dilutes its stake in public-sector enterprises (PSUs).

Types

Minority disinvestment sells a small stake while the government retains control; strategic disinvestment (privatisation) sells a controlling stake and hands over management to a private buyer.

Objectives

Disinvestment aims to raise revenue, improve efficiency, reduce fiscal burden and broaden ownership. A dedicated department (DIPAM) manages the process.

Why it matters for UPSC

The types of disinvestment (minority vs strategic) and its objectives are commonly tested Economy topics.

Frequently asked questions

What is strategic disinvestment?

Selling a controlling stake in a PSU along with management control to a private entity.

Which department manages disinvestment in India?

The Department of Investment and Public Asset Management (DIPAM).
