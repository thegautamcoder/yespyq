Finance Commission of India

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

The Finance Commission is a constitutional body constituted every five years to define financial relations between the Union and the states.

Constitutional basis

It is established under Article 280 by the President and is a constitutional body. It typically has a chairman and four members with fixed qualifications.

Core functions

It recommends the distribution of net tax proceeds between the Centre and states (vertical devolution) and among the states (horizontal devolution), and the principles governing grants-in-aid to states.

Advisory nature

Its recommendations are advisory, not binding on the government, though they are usually accepted. It is central to India's fiscal federalism.

Why it matters for UPSC

Article 280, its constitutional-body status and its advisory-but-not-binding nature are commonly tested, often in statement-based Polity questions.

Frequently asked questions

Under which article is the Finance Commission constituted?

Article 280 of the Constitution.

Are the Finance Commission's recommendations binding?

No. They are advisory in nature, though they are generally accepted by the government.
