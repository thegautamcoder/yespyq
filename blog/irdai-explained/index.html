IRDAI Explained

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) is the statutory body that regulates and develops the insurance industry in India.

Mandate

IRDAI protects the interests of policyholders, regulates insurers and intermediaries, sets standards, and promotes the orderly growth of the insurance sector.

Status

It is a statutory body established under the IRDAI Act, 1999, and is headquartered in Hyderabad.

Why it matters for UPSC

IRDAI's statutory status and role as insurance regulator are commonly tested economy-governance facts.
