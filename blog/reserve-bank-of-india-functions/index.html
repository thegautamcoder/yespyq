Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

The Reserve Bank of India, established in 1935, is the country's central bank and monetary authority.

Monetary authority

The RBI formulates and implements monetary policy to maintain price stability while keeping in mind growth.

Issuer and regulator

It is the sole issuer of currency notes (except the one-rupee note and coins, issued by the government) and regulates and supervises the banking and financial system.

Banker roles

It acts as banker to the government and to banks, manages foreign exchange and public debt, and serves as lender of last resort.

Why it matters for UPSC

The RBI's functions — currency issue, banking regulation, and its role as banker to the government — are commonly tested, often in statement-based questions.

Frequently asked questions

When was the RBI established?

The Reserve Bank of India was established in 1935.

Who issues one-rupee notes and coins in India?

The Government of India issues one-rupee notes and coins; the RBI issues all other currency notes.
