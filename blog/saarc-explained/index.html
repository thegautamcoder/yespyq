SAARC Explained

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

The South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) is a regional intergovernmental organisation of South Asian nations, established in 1985. Its secretariat is in Kathmandu.

Members

SAARC has eight members: Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan and Sri Lanka.

Objectives and challenges

It aims to promote economic and regional integration, but progress has been slowed by political tensions among members. Decisions are taken unanimously.

Why it matters for UPSC

SAARC's members, its 1985 founding and its challenges are frequently tested IR facts.
