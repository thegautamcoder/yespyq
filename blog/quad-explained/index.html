QUAD (Quadrilateral Security Dialogue)

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

The Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (QUAD) is a strategic grouping of four democracies — India, the United States, Japan and Australia — cooperating in the Indo-Pacific region.

Members and focus

The QUAD promotes a free, open and inclusive Indo-Pacific. It works on maritime security, connectivity, critical technologies, climate, health and supply-chain resilience.

Nature

It is an informal strategic forum rather than a formal military alliance, and holds leaders' summits and ministerial meetings. The Malabar naval exercise involves all four members.

Significance

For India, the QUAD supports a rules-based order and balances regional dynamics while advancing practical cooperation.

Why it matters for UPSC

QUAD members, its Indo-Pacific focus and its non-alliance nature are common IR current-affairs questions.

Frequently asked questions

Which countries are members of the QUAD?

India, the United States, Japan and Australia.

Is the QUAD a military alliance?

No. The QUAD is an informal strategic forum, not a formal military alliance.
