Biodiversity and the IUCN Red List

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Biodiversity is the variety of life on Earth — across genes, species and ecosystems. Its conservation status is tracked globally by the IUCN Red List.

Levels of biodiversity

Biodiversity is studied at three levels: genetic diversity, species diversity and ecosystem diversity.

IUCN Red List categories

The IUCN Red List classifies species by extinction risk: Least Concern, Near Threatened, Vulnerable, Endangered, Critically Endangered, Extinct in the Wild and Extinct. It is the most widely used measure of conservation status.

Hotspots in India

India hosts parts of four global biodiversity hotspots — the Himalayas, the Western Ghats, the Indo-Burma region and Sundaland (Nicobar Islands).

Why it matters for UPSC

IUCN Red List categories and India's biodiversity hotspots are among the most reliably tested Environment topics in Prelims.
