The Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) is a regional grouping formed in 1967 to promote economic growth, stability and cooperation in Southeast Asia.

Members

ASEAN comprises ten member states, including Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, the Philippines, Vietnam and others. Its secretariat is in Jakarta.

India and ASEAN

India is a dialogue partner and engages ASEAN centrally through its Act East Policy, cooperating on trade, connectivity and maritime security in the Indo-Pacific.

Why it matters for UPSC

ASEAN's membership, Act East Policy and Indo-Pacific relevance are common IR current-affairs topics.
