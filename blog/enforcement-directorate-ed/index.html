Enforcement Directorate (ED) Explained

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

The Enforcement Directorate (ED) is a law-enforcement and economic-intelligence agency that enforces economic laws to fight financial crime.

Key laws

The ED enforces the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) and the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), investigating money laundering and foreign-exchange violations and attaching proceeds of crime.

Position

It functions under the Department of Revenue in the Ministry of Finance.

Why it matters for UPSC

The ED's role under PMLA and FEMA is a commonly tested governance and economy topic.

Frequently asked questions

Which laws does the Enforcement Directorate enforce?

Mainly the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) and the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA).

Under which ministry does the ED function?

The Ministry of Finance (Department of Revenue).
