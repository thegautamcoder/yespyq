Fundamental Rights (Part III)

By YESPYQ · Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

Fundamental Rights, contained in Part III (Articles 12–35) of the Constitution, are the basic human rights guaranteed to citizens (and some to all persons), enforceable in the courts.

The six categories

The six Fundamental Rights are: Right to Equality (14–18), Right to Freedom (19–22), Right against Exploitation (23–24), Right to Freedom of Religion (25–28), Cultural and Educational Rights (29–30), and the Right to Constitutional Remedies (32).

Justiciable but not absolute

These rights are justiciable — a person can approach the Supreme Court under Article 32 or a High Court under Article 226. However, they are subject to reasonable restrictions and can be suspended during a National Emergency (except Articles 20 and 21).

Heart of the Constitution

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called Article 32 the 'heart and soul' of the Constitution because it makes the rights meaningful by providing a remedy.

Why it matters for UPSC

Fundamental Rights are among the most tested Polity topics — the six categories, which apply to citizens vs all persons, and Article 32 are perennial Prelims material.
