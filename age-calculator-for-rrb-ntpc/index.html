How RRB NTPC age eligibility works

The Railway NTPC age bar is enforced without exception. Aside from the category relaxations set out above, there is no leeway — miss the window by even a day and you drop out for that cycle.

What settles it is your age on the date the notification specifies — not the day you fill the form or write the exam. That single fixed date is what decides eligibility.

Note: the age bars and rules can change from one notification to the next. Use this calculator for a quick estimate, then confirm your eligibility against the official RRB NTPC notification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the RRB NTPC age limit?
The band for most RRB NTPC posts is 18 to 33 years as on the date the notification names. A handful of posts vary, so look up the post-wise age table.
What are the two levels of RRB NTPC?
There is a Graduate Level (a bachelor's degree, ages 18 to 33) and a 12th Level (a Class 12 pass, ages 18 to 30 for most posts). The age limits and eligibility are not the same across the two.
Is there age relaxation for OBC?
Yes: three years for OBC (non-creamy layer) and five for SC/ST, while PwBD get 10 more (General/OBC) or 15 more (SC/ST) over the general limit.
How many times can I attempt RRB NTPC?
There is no cap on attempts — you can sit every cycle as long as you meet the age and qualification requirements.
What qualifications are required?
Graduate Level posts take any bachelor's degree; Undergraduate Level posts need a Class 12 pass. A few roles, like Accounts Clerk or Typist, may also require typing certification.
What is the photo size requirement?
A recent passport-size photo of 20 to 50 KB in JPG, and a signature done on white paper and kept under 20 KB. The exact pixel dimensions appear in each notification.
What is the age limit for RRB Group D?
RRB Group D runs 18 to 33 years for general category as on the notification date, with three extra years for OBC and five for SC/ST. It is a separate exam from NTPC.
