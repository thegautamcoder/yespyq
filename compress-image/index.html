Why compress images?

Government exam portals in India — SSC, UPSC, IBPS, NTA, RRB and the rest — keep upload sizes small, generally 20–200 KB for a photo or document. Trouble is, a photo shot on a phone usually weighs 2–5 MB, far above those ceilings.

By packing the pixels more efficiently, compression brings the file down. Hold the JPEG quality at 70–85% and the screen looks the same, even though the file commonly loses 60–80% of its weight.

Compression vs. resizing — what's the difference?

  • Compression leaves the pixel dimensions untouched and instead drops the encoding quality to trim the file.
  • Resizing alters the actual width × height in pixels, which shrinks the file as well.
  • If a portal caps only the KB, compress. If it caps both KB and dimensions, resize first, then compress.

Frequently asked questions

How do I compress an image online for free?
Load your image into the tool above, nudge the quality slider, then click Download — the lighter file saves at once, with no sign-up.
Does compressing an image reduce quality?
From 70–90% quality the loss is almost impossible to notice; JPEG artefacts only creep in under 50%. For exam-form uploads, 75–85% strikes a good balance of sharpness and size.
Is my image uploaded to a server?
No — HTML5 Canvas compresses everything in your browser, so the image stays on your device and remains fully private.
What image formats are supported?
JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP and HEIC all load fine, and the result is written as JPEG for the widest compatibility.
How much can I reduce the file size?
Cuts of 50–80% are typical with little to see. At 80% quality a 3 MB phone photo often lands under 300 KB.
What quality should I use for exam portals?
For Indian exam portals, 75–80% is a reliable choice — clear enough for identification yet slim enough to slip under 50–100 KB caps.
