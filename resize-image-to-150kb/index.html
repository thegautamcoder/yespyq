Why resize an image to 150 KB?

Here is the nice part: 150 KB leaves you plenty of headroom, so your photo can hold onto its detail and still come out looking sharp — you are not squeezing it down to the point where faces blur or text turns fuzzy.

The reason you need it comes down to the portals. UPSC, SSC, IBPS, SBI, NTA NEET and most exam sites only take images inside a set KB range, and 150 KB is one of the caps you will run into most often. Push a file past that ceiling and the form simply refuses it, so you have to bring the size down before your application will go through.

The old routine — opening up Paint, Photoshop or GIMP — meant installing software and clicking through a fiddly set of steps. This browser-based resizer handles the same task in a single tap, with nothing uploaded, no account and no cost.

Tips for the best result

  • Start with the crispest original you can find — the tool compresses smartly toward the target and keeps as much detail as it can along the way.
  • Shooting an exam photo? Go for a clear, evenly lit shot with a plain white or light backdrop behind you.
  • Trim in close on your subject (leaving just a little margin) before you resize, so more of your pixels survive once you hit the target.
  • If what comes out looks a bit soft, grab a slightly bigger original and run it through once more.
Good to know: your file always comes back as a JPEG — the very format Indian exam and ID portals are looking for — so it should upload without a hitch on your first try.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Resize Image to 150 KB tool free?
Yes, it is completely free. There is no account to create, no watermark on the output, and no cap on how many files you can process.
Is my image kept private?
Completely. Everything runs locally in your browser using HTML5 Canvas, so your file is never sent to a server and never leaves your device.
Does it work on a phone?
Yes. Open the page in Chrome or Safari on any Android phone or iPhone, add your file and download the result — no app to install.
Which file formats can I use?
JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP and HEIC/HEIF on supported browsers. The output is saved as JPEG so it stays compatible with Indian exam and ID portals.
Do I need to install anything?
No. The tool runs entirely in the browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on both desktop and mobile.
Will resizing to 150 KB lower the quality?
The resizer keeps quality as high as it can while meeting the 150 KB target. At 150 KB you have plenty of headroom, so the output usually looks crisp and clean.
Why do exam portals set a KB file-size limit?
Portals like SSC, UPSC, IBPS, NTA and RRB enforce KB caps to keep their databases light and let uploads finish quickly on slow connections. Go over the limit and the upload form rejects the file.
