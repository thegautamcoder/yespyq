Why resize an image to 10 KB?

Here's the usual problem: you go to submit a form on UPSC, SSC, IBPS, SBI or NTA NEET, and the moment your photo is a shade over the portal's KB ceiling, the upload gets rejected. Those portals only take images inside a fixed KB band, and 10 KB is one of the caps they ask for most often — so until the file fits, the application won't move forward.

The fix used to mean opening Paint, Photoshop or GIMP and clicking through a handful of manual steps on software you had to install. This resizer settles it in one click, straight in the browser — nothing gets uploaded, there's no account, and it costs you nothing.

Tips for the best result

  • Feed it the crispest version of the photo you have — the more detail it starts with, the more survives once it's compressed to size.
  • For exam shots, pick a sharp, evenly lit photo shot against a plain white or light backdrop.
  • Trim close to the subject first, leaving just a small border, so the pixels that matter get the most room at 10 KB.
  • Result came out fuzzy? Go back to a bigger original and run it through once more.
Good to know: every file comes out as a JPEG — the exact format Indian exam and ID portals are set up to receive — so it goes through on the first try.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Resize Image to 10 KB tool free?
It is, fully. You won't sign up for anything, the output carries no watermark, and you can run as many files through it as you like.
Is my image kept private?
Totally private. The whole thing happens on your device via HTML5 Canvas — the picture is never sent to any server, so it stays with you.
Does it work on a phone?
It does. Just open this page in Chrome or Safari on your Android phone or iPhone, drop in the file and download what comes out — there's no app to install.
Which file formats can I use?
You can feed it JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP, and HEIC/HEIF where the browser allows it. It hands back a JPEG, which is what Indian exam and ID portals want.
Do I need to install anything?
Nothing at all. It runs right inside your browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — whether you're on a computer or a phone.
Will resizing to 10 KB lower the quality?
The tool holds onto as much sharpness as the 10 KB target allows. A cap this tight does mean some softening you can't get around, but portals still accept the result.
Why do exam portals set a KB file-size limit?
Bodies like SSC, UPSC, IBPS, NTA and RRB cap file sizes in KB so their storage stays lean and uploads go through fast even on a weak connection. Exceed the cap and the form simply won't take your file.
