About the 3.5 × 4.5 cm photo resizer

You'll see 3.5 × 4.5 cm — the same thing as 35 × 45 mm — requested more than any other size across Indian passport, visa and exam paperwork, and at 300 DPI that equals 413 × 531 px. Both numbers count: nail the pixels but miss the file weight (or the other way round) and the government portal bounces your upload, which is the last thing you want when a deadline is closing in.

Nothing about your image travels anywhere. HTML5 Canvas does all the work right on the page, so your snapshots and ID scans never leave the machine in front of you — no server logs, no cloud copies, nothing to worry about.

How to get a clean result:
  • Begin with a sharp, high-resolution shot so the final 413 × 531 px image stays crisp.
  • Trim the frame down to the face first — that protects the quality when it scales.
  • Check the portal's own instructions; several demand a set pixel size and a KB ceiling together.
  • Planning to print? Send it to a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the 3.5 × 4.5 cm photo resizer free?
It costs nothing at all — you won't hit a sign-up wall, a watermark or any limit on how many photos you run through it.
Is my photo kept private?
Fully private. The processing happens right in your browser through HTML5 Canvas, which means the picture never reaches a server and never leaves your device.
Can I use it on a phone?
Absolutely. Load it in Chrome or Safari on any iPhone or Android handset, drop in your photo and save the result — there's nothing to install.
Which file formats can I upload?
Feed it JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP, or HEIC/HEIF on browsers that read them. What comes back is always a JPEG, since that plays nicest with government and ID portals.
Do I have to install anything?
Not a thing. Everything happens inside the browser you already have — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — whether you're on a computer or a phone.
Is the picture cropped for me?
Crop it yourself beforehand if you wish; from there the tool stretches that region to the precise pixel dimensions and file-size window this format calls for.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Sure — an ordinary phone snap does the job. Choose it from your gallery and the tool shapes it to the official specs on its own.
