About the Voter ID photo resizer

For a Voter ID (EPIC) online application, the photo needs to measure 200 × 230 px and stay under 100 KB. Both the dimensions and the weight have to line up, because the portal rejects anything that strays from the official spec — and a bounced file can hold up your registration.

All the processing runs in your browser through HTML5 Canvas, which means your private photos and ID scans never leave the device in front of you. Nothing is stored in the cloud, nothing is logged on a server, and your privacy stays intact.

How to get the cleanest result:
  • Begin with a sharp, brightly lit shot so it still looks good once scaled down.
  • Tighten the crop around the face first to preserve detail.
  • Make sure the final file lands in the 10–100 KB range the portal accepts.
  • If you plan to print it, send it to a 300 DPI printer.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Voter ID photo resizer free?
Yes — it costs nothing at all. You won't hit a sign-up wall, a watermark or any limit on how many photos you run through it.
Is my photo kept private?
Absolutely. The whole process happens on your own machine through HTML5 Canvas, so the picture is never uploaded to any server and never leaves your device.
Can I use it on a phone?
You can. Just open it in Chrome or Safari on any Android phone or iPhone, load your photo and save the output — there's nothing to install.
Which file formats can I upload?
Feed it JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP or HEIC/HEIF on browsers that handle it. Whatever goes in, the result comes out as JPEG, which government and ID portals accept most reliably.
Do I have to install anything?
Not a thing. It runs entirely in the browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — whether you're on a computer or a phone.
Is the picture cropped for me?
Crop it yourself before you process, and the tool then fits that selection to the precise pixel dimensions and weight range this format calls for.
Can I start from a smartphone photo?
Definitely. An everyday phone snap does the job — choose it from your gallery and the tool matches it to the official spec on its own.
