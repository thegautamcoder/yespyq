Why convert PNG to JPG?

PNG is lossless — it preserves every pixel perfectly and can carry transparency. The downside is size: for photographs, PNG files dwarf their JPEG equivalents. An exam photo stored as PNG might weigh 500 KB–2 MB, whereas the very same photo saved as JPEG at 85% quality usually stays under 100 KB.

Indian exam portals — UPSC, SSC, IBPS, NTA, RRB — will almost always take JPEG/JPG and little else. So if your photo is a PNG, you will have to convert it before most application forms accept the upload.

What about transparency?

Transparent pixels have no place in a JPEG. When your PNG carries a see-through background — as logos, screenshots and edited photos often do — the converter paints those regions in the fill colour you select. For exam photos, white is always the safe pick.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert PNG to JPG online for free?
Load your PNG into the tool above, set a quality and a fill colour, then press Download JPG — no software and no sign-up.
Why convert PNG to JPG?
For photos JPEG is far lighter than PNG, and Indian exam portals mostly take only JPG/JPEG. Converting keeps your image both accepted and slim enough to upload.
What happens to transparent areas in my PNG?
JPEG cannot hold transparency, so the tool paints see-through pixels in your chosen fill colour, white by default. White is the right pick for exam photos and ID documents.
Is my image uploaded to a server?
No — HTML5 Canvas runs the entire conversion in your browser, so the file never leaves your device.
Does conversion lose quality?
From 80–95% quality the eye sees no difference. For exam uploads, 80–85% keeps things sharp while holding the size down.
Can I also convert JPG back to PNG?
Yes — our JPG to PNG converter takes care of the reverse.
