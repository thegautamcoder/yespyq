Frequently asked questions

Does resizing to a custom size cost anything?
Not a rupee. You skip the sign-up, you get no watermark stamped on your work, and there is no ceiling on how many images you push through.
Is my image kept private?
Entirely. HTML5 Canvas handles the resize inside your browser, so the picture is never uploaded to any server and stays on your device throughout.
Can I use it on a phone?
You can. Pull up YESPYQ in Chrome or Safari on any iPhone or Android, load your image, and download what comes out — no app required.
Which image formats can I upload?
Send in JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP or HEIC/HEIF (where your browser allows it). What you get back is always a JPEG, the format that sits best with Indian government exam and ID portals.
Is any software installation required?
Not a thing. Everything happens inside the browser you already run — Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari — on a desktop or a phone.
Which DPI is right for printing?
Treat 300 DPI as the safe default for prints like ID cards, exam photos and documents: 1 inch = 300 px, 1 cm ≈ 118 px, 1 mm ≈ 11.8 px. Since the tool counts in exact pixels, enter the pixel figures those conversions give you.
Why does it save as JPEG rather than PNG?
JPEG is what almost every Indian government exam and ID portal wants, and it keeps files far lighter than PNG without losing photo quality — a perfect match for uploading through online forms.
