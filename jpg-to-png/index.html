When should you convert JPG to PNG?

Being lossless, PNG records every pixel just as it is, free of compression artefacts. That makes it a natural fit for screenshots, text-heavy graphics, logos and anything you expect to edit and save again and again.

Do bear in mind that going from JPG to PNG will not claw back quality — the JPEG compression is already baked in. All the conversion does is move the same pixels into a lossless wrapper. The PNG comes out larger, but it holds steady through repeated edits and saves.

Common use cases

  • Importing a photo into design tools such as Canva or Photoshop that handle PNG better
  • Sites and apps that will only take PNG
  • Keeping quality intact across many edit-and-save rounds
  • Setting up for transparency you plan to add later in an editor

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert JPG to PNG online for free?
Load your JPG into the tool above and press Download PNG — the browser converts it on the spot, with no sign-up.
Does converting JPG to PNG improve quality?
No. There is no undoing JPEG compression; the PNG keeps the identical pixels without adding loss, but it cannot restore detail the JPEG already discarded.
Will the PNG be larger than the original JPG?
Yes — lossless storage means more data, so a 200 KB JPG usually ends up a 500 KB–2 MB PNG.
Is my image uploaded to a server?
No — HTML5 Canvas handles the whole conversion in your browser, so the file never leaves your device.
What formats can I upload?
JPG, JPEG and WEBP all work, and the result is always a PNG.
Can I also convert PNG to JPG?
Yes — head to our PNG to JPG converter for the reverse.
